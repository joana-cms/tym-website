My Story

Between Movement and Listening

From a very young age, I have felt most alive through movement. Not necessarily in competition, although I have experienced it, but in the freedom that movement brings. The joy of running after a ball, learning a new technique, connecting with others, giving, sweating, dreaming.

Sport has always been part of my life, but it was never the whole story.

I followed a more traditional academic path, graduating in Industrial Engineering and Management, and later working with large companies and well-known brands that are part of our everyday lives. It was a path shaped by structure, logic, spreadsheets, and a steady rhythm.

And still, there was always another side.

A quieter and more curious side, drawn to what cannot easily be measured or explained. While studying linear algebra, I found myself fascinated by books like The Celestine Prophecy. Between days filled with meetings and numbers, there were evenings dedicated to reading The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari.

These ideas stayed with me. They shaped the way I see life, not as something to control, but as something to listen to.

Movement remained my point of balance. I explored different sports, guided by curiosity, discovery, and well-being. Today, I play tennis. It was through tennis that I found yoga, initially as a recommendation, a way to balance the physical demands on the body.

But the discovery went far beyond that.

Yoga opened a door, physical, emotional, and inner. It naturally led me to Thai Yoga Massage and to a deeper understanding of the body.

Our body holds so much history. So much that we do not hear, simply because we are not used to listening.

More and more, I feel the need to move with intention.
To listen to the body, and not just take it with me.

My Way of Working

My Approach

I see the body as a living ecosystem. Helping others reconnect — with the body, with the breath, with their own rhythm — is what moves me. In Thai Yoga Massage, I create space to feel, to be present, to search for something more true.

What sets my work apart: Custom listening. I don't follow a rigid choreography; I follow what your body tells me through tension and movement.

Integration: The vigor of yoga and the depth of rhythmic touch. A bridge between the technical structure of my background and the intuition of therapeutic practice.

The Experience

What You Can Expect

How is a session?

We work on the floor, on a mattress. The session lasts between 60 to 90 minutes of total surrender to the present moment.

What do people look for?

Reconnection, chronic stress relief, and a break from the fast pace of everyday life.

What can I feel afterwards?

A sense of expansiveness and an internal silence that lasts far beyond the mat.

Do I need experience?

No. It is for you if you value conscious touch and need to reclaim space to breathe.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this for me?

If you feel tension in your body, spend many hours sitting, play sports, or simply want to slow down, improve your sleep, and reconnect with your body, then yes.

Do I need to be flexible?

No. The goal of the massage is precisely to help you regain flexibility. We always work within your comfort limit.

Does the massage hurt?

There may be moments of good pain — that feeling of releasing a knot — but it should never be uncomfortable. Communication is constant.

What clothes should I wear?

You should wear comfortable and flexible clothing (like training or yoga clothes). The massage is done over your clothes.

How long does a session last?

Sessions typically last between 60 and 90 minutes, depending on the body's needs.
