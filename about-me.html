My Story

Between Movement and Listening

Since I was very young, I've felt more alive through movement. Not necessarily in competition, though I also lived it, but in the freedom that movement brings. The joy of running after a ball, of learning a new technique, of interacting, of giving, of sweating, of dreaming.

Sports have always been part of my life, but it was never the complete story.

I followed a more traditional academic path, graduating in Industrial Engineering and Management and, later, working with large companies and brands that are part of our daily lives. It was a journey of structure, logic, excel sheets, and a constant pace.

And yet, another side always existed.

A quieter and more curious side, attracted by that which is not easily measured or explained. While studying linear algebra, I was enchanted by books like The Celestine Prophecy. Between days filled with meetings and numbers, there were nights dedicated to reading The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari.

Those ideas stayed with me. They shaped the way I see life, not as something to control, but as something to listen to.

Movement continued to be my balance point. I explored different sports modalities, guided by curiosity, discovery, and well-being. Today, I play tennis. It was through tennis that I came to yoga, initially as a recommendation, a way to balance the physical demands of the body.

But the discovery was much deeper than that.

Yoga opened a door—physical, emotional, and internal. It naturally led me to Thai Yoga Massage and a deeper understanding of the body.

Our body holds so much history. So much that we don't hear, simply because we are not used to listening.

More and more I feel the need to move with intention.
To listen to the body, and not just take it with me.

How I can help

Today, my work reflects these two worlds.

  • Thai Yoga Massage, a work through the body, touch, and presence
  • Creation of simple and authentic websites for professionals who work with the body

I believe both start from the same place: creating space, clarity, and connection.
