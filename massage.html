The Touch

The Body at Human Scale

Thai Yoga Massage treatments focused on body listening and restoring natural range of motion.

How I Work

My Approach

I see the body as a living system, in constant adaptation. Well-being results not just from correcting symptoms, but from understanding what the body is signaling through tension, discomfort, or limited movement.

My work is based on body listening, understood as a process that requires attention, practice, and sensitivity. It's not about applying automatic solutions, but about observing, adjusting and accompanying what each body needs in each moment.

Each session is adapted to the person. I don't follow a fixed protocol. I work based on the response of the body, respecting limits and seeking to create conditions so that natural movement and function can be progressively recovered.

I integrate different dimensions of body work. Yoga brought me awareness of movement and breath. Thai Yoga Massage added the work of touch, mobilization and tension release. Together, these practices allow for a more complete approach to the body.

I bring a consistent experience from the perspective of someone who has always sought answers in the body for tension, pain and the need for balance. This experience allows me to recognize patterns common and approach each person with attention and respect.

This work is indicated for different situations: for those who spend many hours sitting and accumulate tension; for those who practice sports and seek to improve mobility, recovery and concentration; or for those who feel the need to reduce stress levels and improve rest.

The goal is not to force quick changes, but to support the body in a gradual process of reorganization, with more awareness, mobility and balance.

Ritual

The Massage

Each session is a ritual of presence. We work on the floor, on a comfortable mattress, which allows for a greater range of movements and leverage for stretches.

  • 1. Initial Conversation: We understand where the body asks for attention.
  • 2. Rhythmic Touch: Gentle and rhythmic pressures along the Sen lines.
  • 3. Mobilization: Assisted stretches, similar to passive yoga poses.

There is no rush. The time of the session is respected so that the nervous system can really lower its guard and allow the release of deep tensions.

The Experience

What You Can Expect

How is a session?

We work on the floor, on a futon. The session lasts between 60 to 90 minutes of total surrender to the present moment.

What do they look for?

Reconnection, chronic stress relief and a pause in the accelerated pace of daily life.

What can they feel after?

A sense of amplitude and an internal silence that lasts beyond the table (or in this case, the futon!)

Do I need experience?

No. It's for you if you value conscious touch and need to reclaim space to breathe.

Doubts

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this for me?

If you feel tension in your body, spend many hours sitting, play sports or simply seek to slow down, improve sleep and reconnect with your body, then yes.

Do I need to be flexible?

No. The goal of the massage is precisely to help recover flexibility. We always work within the limit of comfort.

Does the massage hurt?

There may be moments of good pain — that feeling of releasing a knot — but it should never be uncomfortable. The dialogue is constant.

What clothes should I wear?

You should wear comfortable and flexible clothing (like training or yoga clothes). The massage is done over the clothes.

How long does a session last?

Sessions typically last between 60 and 90 minutes, depending on the body's needs.
