The Touch

The body at human scale

Thai Yoga Massage treatments focused on body listening and restoring natural range of motion.

Approach

Touch as a form of listening

Often, the body screams before we even know we are tired. My approach is not about "fixing" something that is broken, but about listening to what the body has to say through its tension.

In Thai Yoga Massage, we don't just work on muscles; we work on memories and silences. It is a rhythmic dialogue where my role is to create the necessary space for your body to remember how to move freely.

Ritual

How I Work

Each session is a ritual of presence. We work on the floor, on a comfortable mattress, which allows for a greater range of movement and leverage for stretches.

  • 1. Initial Conversation: We understand where the body is asking for attention.
  • 2. Rhythmic Touch: Gentle and rhythmic pressure along the Sen lines.
  • 3. Mobilization: Assisted stretches, similar to passive yoga poses.

There is no rush. The time of the session is respected so that the nervous system can truly lower its guard and allow the release of deep tensions.

Trust

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is this for me?

If you feel tension in your body, spend many hours sitting, play sports, or simply want to slow down, improve your sleep, and reconnect with your body, then yes.


Do I need to be flexible?

No. The goal of the massage is precisely to help you regain flexibility. We always work within your comfort limit.

Does the massage hurt?

There may be moments of good pain — that feeling of releasing a knot — but it should never be uncomfortable. Communication is constant.


What clothes should I wear?

You should wear comfortable and flexible clothing (like training or yoga clothes). The massage is done over your clothes.


How long does a session last?

Sessions typically last between 60 and 90 minutes, depending on the body's needs.
