The Substance of the Path

Where paths cross

Since I was a child, sports were my way of reading the world. Not through the cold geometry of the podium, but through the clumsy joy of movement. It was a well-being that required no explanation, like the laughter of a child discovering gravity.

Time, that beast that forces us to stay still, sat me down. Between studying and creating invisible architectures on the screen, my body became a shelf of forgotten books: rigid, heavy with dust and tension. I lacked the air that only movement knows how to blow.

I found Yoga through anatomical curiosity, and it led me to Thai Yoga Massage. It was like finding the umbrella we had lost on an afternoon of ancient rain.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is flexibility required?

No. The massage is adapted to your body. The goal is precisely to increase your flexibility progressively.

What clothes should I bring?

Comfortable and loose clothing (sporty or yoga style) that allows legs and arms to move freely.

Does it hurt?

The massage can be intense, but it should never be painful. We maintain constant communication to adjust pressure to your limit.
